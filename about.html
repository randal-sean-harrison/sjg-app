The Social Justice Game: A Game About Cents & Sensibility®, offers an educational parody of the game Monopoly® in order to demonstrate the systemic nature of povery. This ‘serious’ game helps players experience systemic inequity in an embodied way, and is designed so that players are encouraged to reevaluate and complicate their own understanding of poverty, ro reisist overl simplistic, social darwinist notions that the poor are lazy, or in some way less deserving, less human.

Unlike Monopoly®, which assumes a level socioeconomic playing field, The game more closely reflects actual inequities under the current American economy. It leverages players' prior experience playing Monopoly by forcing them to pit their own expectations of 'fairness' against the economic pressures they experience in the game.
